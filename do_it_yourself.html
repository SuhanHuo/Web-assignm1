DO IT YOURSLEF!

prepare: a piece of paper & a pen

(1) Please recall a recent situation that made you anxious and write about the trigger that triggered your anxiety.

Example: You ate a snack while doing your homework at 11 o 'clock last night. Unconsciously you ate three packets of potato chips. After eating, you suddenly felt that you must become particularly fat and began to worry. Trigger: Too many late-night snacks

(3) Please write down the cognitive logic behind anxiety

Example: You think that eating three packets of potato chips at midnight will make you very, very fat and you will never lose weight again.

(5) Please write down your improved thoughts after the future occurrence of the trigger that makes you anxious

Example: Eating three packets of potato chips at a time may make you feel a little upset when you go to bed tonight, but as long as you exercise and eat properly tomorrow, this little binge is actually nothing.

(2) Please recall what emotional and physical changes occurred to you during the situation.

Example: Your mind is tense, your heart is racing, and you hate and hate yourself.

(4) Write down another thought that challenges your cognitive logic

In fact, three packets of potato chips will not immediately become fat, only eat this every night, lasting one or two months will become a lot of weight. In fact, you weigh less than 55 under the height of 170, even if a little fat is good.

(6) Finally, please re-rate your anxiety after having improved thinking on a scale of 0-100!
